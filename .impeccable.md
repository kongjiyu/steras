## Design Context

### Users

STERAS serves Malaysian event organizers and authority reviewers from PDRM, Bomba, KKM, DBKL, and MOTAC. Organizers submit and track tourism event permit applications. Authority officers review the authoritative final risk, inspect its deterministic baseline and bounded M3 adjustment, verify recommended resources, and record auditable decisions. The current design priority is the Authority Dashboard, optimized for repeated desktop review work while remaining fully usable on mobile.

### Brand Personality

Malaysian, tourism-oriented, and modern. The interface should feel locally grounded, trustworthy, calm, and operationally capable. It should give officers confidence that important risk information is legible and evidence based, while retaining the warmth and civic identity of Malaysian tourism.

### Aesthetic Direction

Use the warm cream and olive-green direction established by `design-sample.png`, with restrained gold accents and clear semantic risk colors. Authority operations use the **Civic Observatory** expression: a warm light workspace, deep olive operational bands, fine woven linework, and data-driven radar or route motifs. Signature visuals must explain live portfolio or coverage state rather than act as decoration. Favor a refined civic workspace over a promotional tourism site: crisp typography, quiet surfaces, strong information hierarchy, and subtle Malaysian visual references. Avoid generic SaaS gradients, glass-heavy styling, oversized marketing elements, excessive rounded cards, and decorative effects that compete with safety-critical data. Use a light theme first and meet WCAG AA contrast where practical.

### Design Principles

1. Make the next review action and highest-risk information immediately scannable.
2. Use Malaysian identity through color, language, and restrained visual motifs rather than ornament.
3. Present final risk first, then distinguish the deterministic baseline, bounded M3 advice, and authority decisions clearly.
4. Keep dense operational screens calm, consistent, and accessible.
5. Adapt workflows for mobile without hiding critical information or actions.
6. Reuse shared semantic components and tokens before creating page-specific treatments.

### Reusable System Contracts

- `StatusBadge` represents workflow state only. `RiskMeter` represents Low, Medium, and High assessed risk everywhere.
- RiskMeter uses one, two, or three ascending ticks plus a visible text label. Use compact size in tables and hero lockups, default size in details, surface tone on light backgrounds, and inverse tone on deep olive.
- Keep the final numeric score adjacent to RiskMeter. On narrow layouts, stack this risk lockup below the full event name instead of truncating critical context.
- Use one coordinated entrance sequence per page and functional state feedback from the shared motion tokens. Avoid decorative loops and honor `prefers-reduced-motion`.
- Treat woven, radar, route, and tourism imagery as information-bearing identity elements, not ornamental filler.
